## Assessment Overview

### Assessment Philosophy

The CLMS training assessment program is designed to:
- Validate competency in system operation
- Identify areas for additional training
- Ensure high-quality library service delivery
- Provide objective measures of skill development
- Support continuous improvement
- Build confidence in system usage
- Maintain consistent service standards
- Support professional development goals

### Assessment Types

#### Formative Assessments
- **Purpose**: Monitor learning progress during training
- **Timing**: Throughout training sessions
- **Format**: Quizzes, observations, practice exercises
- **Feedback**: Immediate and constructive
- **Goal**: Identify learning gaps and adjust training

#### Summative Assessments
- **Purpose**: Evaluate overall training effectiveness
- **Timing**: End of training modules/program
- **Format**: Comprehensive tests, practical demonstrations
- **Results**: Pass/fail determination, competency certification
- **Goal**: Validate achievement of learning objectives

#### Performance Assessments
- **Purpose**: Evaluate on-the-job performance
- **Timing**: Regular intervals after training
- **Format**: Observations, customer feedback, metrics
- **Outcomes**: Performance improvement plans
- **Goal**: Ensure sustained competency and quality

### Assessment Levels

#### Level 1: Reaction
- **What**: Trainee satisfaction with training
- **How**: Surveys, feedback forms
- **When**: Immediately after training
- **Why**: Evaluate training relevance and delivery

#### Level 2: Learning
- **What**: Knowledge and skill acquisition
- **How**: Tests, demonstrations, practical exercises
- **When**: During and after training
- **Why**: Measure learning achievement

#### Level 3: Behavior
- **What**: Application of skills on the job
- **How**: Observations, performance metrics
- **When**: Weeks to months after training
- **Why**: Evaluate transfer of learning

#### Level 4: Results
- **What**: Impact on library operations
- **How**: Performance metrics, customer satisfaction
- **When**: Months after training
- **Why**: Measure organizational impact

## Assessment Scoring and Criteria

### Scoring Guidelines

#### Passing Scores
```
Basic Knowledge Tests:
• Minimum passing score: 80%
• Retest allowed after 1 week
• Maximum of 3 attempts
• Required for certification

Practical Assessments:
• Minimum passing score: 85%
• Direct observation required
• Must demonstrate consistency
• No retest without additional training

Customer Service Evaluation:
• Minimum rating: 3 out of 5
• Must meet all critical criteria
• Professional conduct required
• No retest without improvement plan
```

#### Performance Levels
```
Outstanding (90-100%):
• Exceeds expectations consistently
• Demonstrates mastery
```
